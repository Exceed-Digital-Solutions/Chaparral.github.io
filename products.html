Framed Wall Mirror

A polished edge mirror refers to a type of mirror where the edges have been finely polished to achieve a smooth and sleek appearance. Unlike a standard straight-cut edge, which can be rough or sharp, a polished edge adds a touch of elegance and refinement to the mirror.

Here are some key features and benefits of a polished edge mirror:

  • Appearance: The polished edges give the mirror a clean and finished look. The smooth edges enhance the overall aesthetics of the mirror, making it more visually appealing.


  • Safety: Polished edges are less likely to be sharp or cause injuries compared to rough-cut edges. This is particularly important when the mirror is mounted or installed in locations where it may be touched or handled frequently.


  • Easy Handling: The polished edges make the mirror easier to handle during installation. The smooth surface reduces the risk of accidental damage or injury while maneuvering the mirror.


  • Versatility: Polished edge mirrors can be used in a variety of applications, including bathrooms, dressing rooms, home decor, commercial settings, and more. Their sleek appearance complements various interior styles and designs.


  • Customization: Chaparral offers a range of thickness options for polished edge mirrors to accommodate specific needs and preferences. Custom shapes and sizes are often available as well.

A beveled edge mirror is a type of mirror where the edges have been cut and shaped at an angle, creating a decorative effect. Instead of having a straight and flat edge, the edges of a beveled edge mirror are slanted and usually have a polished finish.

Here are some key features and benefits of a beveled edge mirror:

Decorative Appearance: The beveled edges add a touch of elegance and sophistication to the mirror. The angled edges create a framed look, giving the mirror a distinct and stylish appearance.

  • Light Reflection: The beveled edges help to reflect light in interesting ways. The angled surfaces catch and reflect light, creating a subtle play of light and shadow, which can enhance the overall visual impact of the mirror.


  • Depth and Dimension: The beveled edges can give the illusion of depth and dimension to the mirror. The slanted edges create a sense of depth, making the mirror appear more substantial and visually appealing.


  • Versatility: Beveled edge mirrors can be used in various settings and design styles, including bathrooms, living rooms, hallways, and even as decorative wall pieces. They can complement both traditional and contemporary interior designs.


  • Customization: Chaparral offers a range of bevel widths, allowing you to choose the desired level of beveling for your mirror. Additionally, custom shapes and sizes can be created to suit your specific requirements.


  • It is important to note that the beveled edges on a mirror can make it slightly more delicate and vulnerable to chipping compared to mirrors with straight-cut edges. Care should be taken during handling and installation to avoid damaging the beveled edges.
